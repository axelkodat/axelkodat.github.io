track information

  1. Anderson's reference is "Quodlibet for solo dambura," Track A6 on the 1971 release An Anthology Of The World's Music: Afghanistan III from Anthology Records. The performer is credited as Bābā Qerān, though as Anderson observes this simply means "old man" in Kurdish. This album was assembled from field recordings made by Mark Slobin in 1968, and there's lots of interesting background on the project on his website. The track linked above is actually Track 1-9, "Dambura Medley," from a later compilation, Afghanistan Untouched, released (rather ironically given the title) in 2003 on the Traditional Crossroads label. This album was also assembled from Slobin's 1968 recordings, and again the track is credited to Bābā Qerān. Since the titles are synonymous (and estimating from available metadata, the track lengths are about the same), this is almost certainly the same recording as on the older LP.
